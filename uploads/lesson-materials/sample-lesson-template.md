# Sample Lesson Plan Template

## Lesson Information
- **Title:** Introduction to Cell Biology
- **Grade Level:** 9th Grade
- **Subject:** Biology
- **Duration:** 60 minutes
- **Standards Alignment:** NGSS HS-LS1-1, HS-LS1-2

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Identify the main parts of a cell and describe their functions
2. Compare and contrast plant and animal cells
3. Explain the relationship between cell structure and function

## Materials Needed
- Cell diagram handouts
- Microscopes (1 per group)
- Prepared slides of plant and animal cells
- Colored pencils
- Worksheets
- Interactive whiteboard

## Warm-up (5-10 minutes)
Begin with a quick drawing activity: ask students to draw what they think a cell looks like inside. Have a few students share their drawings with the class.

## Main Lesson (25 minutes)
1. Present a brief overview of cell theory and the history of cell discovery
2. Show images of different types of cells and discuss their similarities and differences
3. Use interactive models to demonstrate the structure and function of:
   - Cell membrane
   - Nucleus
   - Mitochondria
   - Chloroplasts (in plant cells)
   - Endoplasmic reticulum
   - Golgi apparatus
4. Compare plant and animal cells using a Venn diagram

## Guided Practice (15 minutes)
1. Divide students into groups of 3-4
2. Each group will examine prepared slides under microscopes
3. Students will draw what they observe and label cell parts
4. Students will complete a worksheet comparing plant and animal cells

## Independent Practice/Assessment (10 minutes)
Students will complete a cell analogy activity, relating cell parts to components of a city or factory and explaining why the analogy is appropriate.

## Differentiation
- **For advanced students:** Research specialized cells and their adaptations
- **For struggling students:** Provide a simplified diagram with fewer cell parts to identify
- **For ELL students:** Provide visual glossary with key vocabulary

## Closure (5 minutes)
Exit ticket: Have students write down three things they learned about cells and one question they still have.

## Homework/Extension
Students will research a specialized cell type (neuron, muscle cell, etc.) and create a model showing how its structure supports its function.

## Assessment
- Participation in class discussions
- Completion of cell observation worksheet
- Exit ticket responses
- Cell analogy activity
